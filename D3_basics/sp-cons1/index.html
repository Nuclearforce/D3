17 in every 100 South Africans 25-64 years old hold a post school credential. Since 2009, credential attainment has increased by 3.3 percentage points. Although credential attainment has seen progress, urgent action is required to up-skill the nation and reach the goals set in the National Development Plan.

What does educational attainment look like in South Africa?
With the fourth industrial revolution looming, South African President Cyril Ramaphosa has advocated that now, more than ever before, there is a need to skill and re-skill the nation to take and to make job opportunities in the labour market. Unfortunately, fewer than 15% of South Africans have a post school credential.

Credential attainment by province
Among working-age South Africans (25-64 years), attainment of credentials varies across provinces. The Western Cape and Gauteng provinces have a higher proportion of citizens with a post school credential, 20% and 25% respectively, in comparison to other provinces in the country.
